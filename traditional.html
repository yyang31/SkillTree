Self-Growth (SG)

  1. Shows enthusiasm and energy for seeking growth.
  2. Seek opportunities for learning and development.
  3. Seek resources for learning and development.
  4. Demonstrate a commitment for self-growth.
  5. Apply new skills on the job.
  6. Seek resources for learning and development.
  7. Share new insights and knowledge with others.
  8. Use opportunities to expand abilities connected to organizational needs.
  9. Demonstrate a strong, long-term commitment for self-growth.
  10. Create a detailed development plan.
  11. Create a thorough development plan that factors in organizational and industry dynamics.
  12. Pursue ways to enhance skills and advance organizational strategy.
  13. Motivate others to continuously grow and learn.
  14. Create a culture that strongly values and enhances individual and career development.

Adaptability (AD)

  1. Change how one is presented based on the situation.
  2. Understand social cues in varying contexts.
  3. Exemplifies resourcefulness by responding to a variety of challenges and situations.
  4. Swiftly and easily change how one is presented based on the situation.
  5. Adopts new behaviors according to specific cues presented in context.
  6. Practice persistence and flexibility.
  7. Considers the needs of clients, constituents, and organizations.
  8. Responds deftly to a variety of challenges and situations.
  9. Help others adapt to new situations.
  10. Builds structures and culture that allow organizational flexibility.
  11. Help others swiftly adapt and adjust to necessary factors.
  12. Builds structures and culture that changes external environment and drives changes in response.

Balances Stakeholders (B)

  1. Learn about various stakeholders and gain insight.
  2. Listens to others to gain an understanding of various stakeholder priorities.
  3. Takes initiative to respond to stakeholder concerns and issues.
  4. Engages effectively with multiple stakeholders.
  5. Probes deeper to gain richer details and backgrounds about stakeholders.
  6. Understand and respect different cultures and values of different groups.
  7. Respond to stakeholders with well-balanced, win-win solutions.
  8. Ensures people understand and adhere to ethical standards when working with stakeholders.
  9. Maximize outcomes for all stakeholders and identify all relevant issues.
  10. Promotes and environment of high ethical standards.
  11. Navigate multiple cultural spaces with respect and professionalism for each of their norms and values.
  12. Create, embody, and enforce organizational culture in which people follow the highest ethics and deepest cultural sensitivity.

Communication (C)

  1. Pay attention to others' insights, advice, or instructions to grasp main concepts and pieces.
  2. Promptly shares relevant info with right amount of detail.
  3. Gives appropriate context and details when speaking.
  4. Gives appropriate context and details when writing.
  5. Listens carefully and probes beneath the surface to gain richer insight on others' views.
  6. Produces rich, professional documents and reports that widely shared internally and externally of the organization.
  7. Creates forums for people to express themselves openly and regularly.
  8. Delivers messages effectively to all levels of the organization, tailoring communication content and style to the needs of others.
  9. Pays attention to others' input and perspectives, asks questions, and summarizes to confirm understanding.
  10. Has a strong, compelling impact on people at all levels, building interest and commitment and creating culture of ongoing constructive dialogue.

Interconnectivity (I)

  1. Listens and responds non-defensively when given advice, instruction, or feedback.
  2. Maintains an open, accepting demeanor when interacting with others.
  3. Grasp others' more obvious social cues and responds appropriately.
  4. Shows sensible reactions and sensitivity in difficult interpersonal situations.
  5. Grasp others' subtle social cues and nimbly responds to others' needs and concerns.
  6. Starts conversations and builds rapport with audience members.
  7. Builds rapport with ease.
  8. Helps to diffuse difficult interpersonal situations by showing high levels of tact, sensitivity, and consideration.
  9. Works to build greater group harmony.
  10. Coach members on interpersonal skills.
  11. Understands the nuances of interpersonal group dynamics and leverages this knowledge to achieve results.
  12. Takes time to build rapport in meetings and groups and speaks about common interests with poise and polish.
  13. Picks up on team interpersonal dynamics and finds ways to work effectively and resolve interpersonal conflict, internal and external to the organization.
  14. Cultivates a culture in which interpersonal and group dynamics are positive internally and externally.

Ambiguity (AM)

  1. Connecting with appropriate individuals to gain direction.
  2. Takes steps forward, despite unknown details.
  3. Helps others deal with uncertainty or lack of info.
  4. Look for help on how to adapt to changes and responds with calm composure.
  5. Ask questions to anticipate new priorities.
  6. Offer coaching and suggestions to help individuals navigate ambiguity and continue moving forward.
  7. Responding effectively to unclear situations by resolving ambiguity and make progress.
  8. Remain calm, positive, and focused during transitions or chaotic situations.
  9. Steer others skillfully with a stabilizing presence and clear targeted instruction in order to make progress.
  10. Discerns and capitalizes on promising, ambiguous situations to manage change.
  11. Champions change by rallying and inspiring people to take swift and effective action, offering a clear path forward during ambiguous times.

Alignment (AL)

  1. Meets deadlines on time with acceptable quality of work.
  2. Point out available support and how can they be used to carry out plans.
  3. Identify available resources and how can they be used to carry out plans.
  4. Deliver efficient, high-quality work.
  5. Skillfully identify and use available resources.
  6. Skillfully identify and use available support and explore options.
  7. Incorporate new activities that allow for optimal efficiency and effective coordination.
  8. Uphold strategic objectives with aggressive yet realistic time frames.
  9. Drives actions to coordinate and align strategic goals.
  10. Incorporate new activities to coordinate and align plans across workgroups or departments.
  11. Generate comprehensive and exact goals to produce clear organizational priorities.
  12. Swiftly drives actions to coordinate and align strategic goals to build sense of urgency.

Strategic Mindset (SM)

  1. Spots what is gaining traction and asks about future issues that may impact one's work.
  2. Understand and articulates how one's actions contribute to organizational success and prioritizes actions towards goals.
  3. Explores possibilities that can impact the team or organization in the future.
  4. Sparks discussion about new trends and how these impact the work, worker, industry, or consumers.
  5. Takes industry, market trends, and organizational goals into account when making decisions.
  6. Effectively combines long-term opportunities and challenges with daily activities.
  7. Identifies and prioritizes efforts and initiatives to have the greatest impacts on the organization.
  8. Keeps updated on current and future industry trends and market forces.
  9. Develops strong, long-term strategies that capitalize on the organization's unique capabilities and on the emerging market trends.
  10. Develops a clear picture of organizational vision and strategy and what needs to be done to accomplish them.
  11. Is a strong big-picture thinker and makes frequent, clear references to organizational vision and strategy with necessary stepping stones.
  12. Is highly alert to the future, analyzing multiple possibilities and scenarios to equip the organization to the address change, tackle, challenge, and shape new opportunities.

Outcomes Driven (O)

  1. Maintains concentration on work, despite obstacles or setbacks.
  2. Completes tasks consistently in a timely manner.
  3. Maintains productivity and positive attitude.
  4. Focuses on making and completing key goals.
  5. Sticks with goals to completion or initiatives in a timely way, despite setbacks or obstacles.
  6. Provide assistance and encouragement to help others over obstacles.
  7. Drives ahead with great energy when faced with obstacles and setbacks.
  8. Ensures the team pushes through obstacles and establishes superior track record by creating feelings of energy and productive behaviors.
  9. Champions a result-focused culture based on meaningful tasks and energetic action, such as inspiring others and overcoming challenges
